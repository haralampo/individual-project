“folklore” marked a departure from her usual album release patterns as Taylor surprised fans by announcing and releasing the album on the same day. Crafted during the COVID-19 lockdown, the album delved into narratives rich with nostalgia, fictional tales, and personal reflections. The album immediately captivated listeners with its haunting melodies, introspective storytelling, and unexpected indie-folk sound. Her gift of storytelling shone through as she created intricate characters and vivid landscapes within each song; three of the songs are connected to form the tale of a love triangle.

The album received critical acclaim, commended as a masterpiece for its sonic departure and lyrical depth. “folklore” won Album of the Year at the Grammys, making Taylor the only female to win the honor three times… and she did it in three different genres in three different decades! Swift's ability to effortlessly pivot genres solidified her status as a boundary-pushing artist in the music industry.
