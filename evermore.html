The "evermore" album was released just 6 months after its sister album, "folklore," extending Taylor's journey into the realm of indie-folk storytelling. Similarly, it was released the same day that it was announced. The album maintained the haunting melodies and intricate storytelling that defined its predecessor, and delved further into character-driven tales. Many fans feel that folklore represents grief and pain while evermore represents reflection and healing.
