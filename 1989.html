“1989” marked a complete shift in Taylor’s musical trajectory as it was her first fully pop album with zero traces of country influence. Taylor channeled the sounds of the synth-pop from the late '80s and created hits such as “Blank Space” and “Style.” The album encapsulated themes of self-discovery, independence, and the rollercoaster of emotions one faces in their twenties. During this era, Taylor chopped her hair and moved to New York City, further embracing the concept of change and experimentation and being who you really are. She learned to have confidence in herself and ignore the whispers behind her back scrutinizing her dating life and criticizing her for “ditching” country music.

"1989" skyrocketed to number one on the Billboard 200, selling over a million copies in its debut week. The subsequent "1989 World Tour" reinforced her global superstar status, featuring elaborate performances and surprise guests including Selena Gomez, Chris Rock, and Mick Jagger. 1989 won Album of the Year at the Grammys, making Taylor the first female artist to win the honor twice. This album solidified her as a dominant force in pop music, demonstrating her versatility as a songwriter and artist.
